This document records the sampled editorial audit behind the concordance 1.1.0 coverage claims (`story-packages/concordance/eternal-return.v1.json`) and feeds the canon contradiction register (`story-packages/concordance/contradictions.v1.json`). Contradictions are recorded for owner decision, never silently fixed (ADR 0002).

The completed audits are cached in the workflow journal (run `wf_2aab68cc-3e9`), so a future resume replays them at no cost and runs only the remaining 14 family audits plus the adversarial verification passes. Findings listed below are **unverified candidates** until the verification pass confirms them and they enter the register. The Batch 5.1 coverage model (every identity class classified) is complete and enforced independently of this audit; the audit is the sampled editorial evidence behind the family-level claims and must finish before Batch 5.3's no-open-sev-1 gate can be evaluated.

## Method

- **Denominator:** every variation family in the shipped `eternal-return@1.1.0` catalog — 19 families covering 1,014 variations — plus all three endings, audited against the family's concordance mapping and the canonical rule system at manuscript commit `6720e76…` (the accepted literary release's `sourceCommit`; read-only local clone).
- **Sampling (84 items, recorded per family in the concordance `sampledVariationIds`):** all 12 L1 variations; 5 of 81 per L2 family (`-001, -021, -041, -061, -081`); 5 of 45 per character L3 family (`-001, -012, -023, -034, -045`); 9 of 135 conv-L3 fragments (three per `philosophyDominant` framing: accept 001–003, resist 007–009, invest 019–021); all 3 endings in full.
- **Rubric per sampled item:** the Four Shackles (identity/opposition/analogy/resemblance); the four forbidden narrative moves (transmission metaphors, planner/foresight explanations, unified-I after dissolution — scoped to L3/L4 content, transcendence-through-escape, plus the dramatize-never-explain rule); the mapped narrator's voice fingerprint including both cross-contamination blacklists (with designed dissolution-era bleed licensed in L3/L4 and second-person/reader-address framing treated as a permitted edition difference per ADR 0002); chronology/era conformance (near/mid/deep future assignments, slow-bleed catastrophe, no functioning deep-future technology); controlled terminology (Augenblick as rupture, pharmakon dual valence, differentiation/differenciation, protocol completeness per era); canonical names (role-names, "Architect", the supporting cast, no invented proper names); and aptness of the mapping's relationship class, constraint IDs, and promise IDs for what the prose actually does.
- **Designed-repetition allowlist:** the 15 sensory rhymes, phrase bleeding ("I find myself" / "I find myself found"), and the shared sentence "The form is what makes self-observation possible" are never reported as duplication.
- **Adversarial verification:** every candidate finding goes to an independent skeptic prompted to refute it (inaccurate quote, designed repetition, permitted interactive difference, licensed dissolution bleed, or a misread rule) before it can enter the contradiction register. Nothing below enters the register unverified.

## Interim results (unverified candidates, pending the adversarial pass)

All five completed family audits assess their mapping's claims as **not fully holding** (`claimsHold: false`) — the relationship class holds in each case, but chronology, promise, and voice claims show real gaps. The two completed L2 audits repeat the L1 patterns almost exactly (era naming, absolute dates, protagonist gender, voice contamination, promise over-claims), which suggests systemic authoring-generation artifacts rather than isolated slips — directly relevant to how the #156 content release should be scoped. Recurring cross-family patterns:

1. **Era/terminology drift (sev-1 candidates).** Runtime L1 prose asserts era-state facts that contradict the canonical worldbuilding: "Upload Era" and an Archive still under construction in the Archaeologist's era (canon: Integration Era; the Integration industry already dominant; the near-future database is the Deep Architecture); hard-pinned absolute years and compressed century-scale spacing between threads (canon keeps relative, longer spans); an operational archive with hundreds of thousands of intact consciousnesses in the Last Human's era (canon: the database failed); invented era/institution names ("Great Aggregation", "the Great Archive", "Consciousness Archival Facility 2276") absent from the name index.
2. **A Four Shackles candidate (sev-1).** One hum-L1 variation declares the three temporal positions a single consciousness in non-dream content — an identity-shackle violation if the verification pass confirms the reading.
3. **Forbidden-move exposition (sev-1 candidate).** arch-L1-003 explains the bootstrap paradox at length in expository narration (the rule is dramatize, never explain); the same passage avoids the planner error, which the owner should weigh.
4. **Concordance metadata errors the audit itself exposed (sev-2).** The 1.0.0 mappings use `er-chronology-phase-N` as if the IDs meant narrative movements, but the release's chronology IDs denote catastrophe _eras_ (phase-1 is the Archaeologist's era; phase-2 the between-eras Exodus gap) — so algorithm/last-human L1 families and all archaeologist L2 families carry the wrong era tags. Several declared promise chains over-claim: promises with zero presence anywhere in a fully-covered family (e.g. only P5 of six claimed promises is planted in arch-L1; the Martinez and Lena chains claimed on both audited arch-L2 families have no presence in their prose).
5. **Voice-fingerprint contamination (sev-2).** Algorithm-pattern texture (percentages, precise timestamps, nested conditionals, analytical framing) appears in archaeologist and last-human L1 prose, where no designed-bleed license exists.
6. **Family-internal continuity breaks (sev-2).** Initial and revisit variations authored from divergent premises (different fragment identities, awareness regressions, protagonist gender split against canon).
